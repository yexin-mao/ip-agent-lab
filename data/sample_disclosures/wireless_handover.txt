Title: Predictive low-latency handover method for dense 5G networks

Technical Field:
This invention relates to wireless communication, especially mobility management and handover optimization in dense 5G and future 6G radio access networks.

Background:
In dense cellular networks, user equipment may rapidly move across small cells. Conventional handover is often triggered after signal quality degradation is already observed, which may lead to packet loss, service interruption, and latency spikes for real-time applications.

Technical Solution:
The proposed method collects beam measurement reports, user mobility history, application latency requirements, and serving cell load. A prediction module estimates near-future link degradation and generates a ranked list of candidate target beams and cells. The serving base station preconfigures radio resources for the most likely target cell before the handover command is executed.

Innovation Points:
1. Predicting link degradation before conventional handover thresholds are crossed.
2. Combining beam quality, mobility history, latency requirements, and serving cell load in one target selection score.
3. Preconfiguring target cell resources to reduce handover interruption time.

Technical Effects:
The method reduces handover delay, lowers packet loss, and improves service continuity for ultra low latency applications.

Applications:
Dense 5G networks, industrial IoT, high speed mobility, augmented reality communication, and private wireless networks.
